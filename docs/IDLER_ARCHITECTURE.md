# Idler Architecture

## Operating definition

The modeled wheel is a passive idler for a crawler chassis that moves in both
directions. The tooth profile is therefore symmetric. The idler carries and
indexes the track but does not transmit propulsion torque.

## Assembly order

1. Place the first rigid side plate on the assembly fixture.
2. Install the inner registration ring.
3. Position all nineteen cured cast tooth modules against the ring.
4. Insert the nineteen valley liners axially into the completed slots.
5. Install the second rigid side plate.
6. Pass threaded rods through the plate and module holes.
7. Clamp the assembly evenly.
8. Attach the selected metal hub to the reference bolt pattern.
9. Mount the rotating shaft in external chassis bearings.

## Functional separation

- Track geometry is defined by the accepted open-valley profile.
- Wear is handled by replaceable liners.
- Casting provides local backing under the teeth and valleys.
- Fiberglass rods provide local crack-control reinforcement.
- Threaded rods clamp the wheel assembly.
- Side plates establish axial alignment and retain the liners.
- The registration ring establishes radial module position.
- The metal hub and shaft establish the rotating axis.
- External bearings establish the precision bearing interface.

## First-attempt limitations

This batch does not yet include:

- side-plate lightening patterns;
- segmented side plates or registration rings;
- exact commercial hub or bearing dimensions;
- mold rim clips for holding fiberglass rods;
- liner texture, grooves, or secondary retention pins;
- concrete volume minimization inside each module; or
- 32-inch and 48-inch architecture presets.

Those decisions follow physical inspection of the Batch 004 geometry.
